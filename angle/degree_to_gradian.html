Understanding Degrees and Gradians

What is a Degree?

A degree (¬∞) is a measurement of a plane angle, representing 1/360 of a full rotation. It is a commonly used unit in geometry, navigation, and everyday life for describing the size of an angle.

What is a Gradian?

A gradian (grad), also known as the gon, is a unit of plane angle, equivalent to 1/400 of a full circle. This means a right angle is 100 gradians. It is a metric unit of angle, but less common than degrees and radians.
