Understanding Hertz and RPM

What is a Hertz (Hz)?

The Hertz (Hz) is the SI unit of frequency, defined as one cycle per second. It is the standard unit for measuring any periodic event, including rotational speed.

What is RPM?

Revolutions per minute (RPM) is a unit of frequency expressing the number of full rotations completed in one minute around a fixed axis. It is a common measure of rotational speed in mechanical engineering and automotive applications.

Why Convert Hz to RPM?

Converting Hertz to RPM is useful for translating scientific frequency measurements into a more common unit for rotational speed. This is essential in engineering and automotive contexts where RPM is the standard. Our converter performs this conversion accurately using the formula: 1 Hz = 60 RPM.
