Understanding Hertz and Gigahertz

What is a Hertz (Hz)?

The Hertz (Hz) is the SI unit of frequency, defined as one cycle per second. It is used to measure the frequency of everything from sound waves and light to computer processor speeds.

What is a Gigahertz (GHz)?

A Gigahertz (GHz) is a unit of frequency equal to one billion Hertz (1,000,000,000 Hz). It is widely used to specify the clock speed of computer processors, the frequency of satellite communications, and various other high-frequency applications.

Why Convert Hz to GHz?

Converting Hertz to Gigahertz is necessary for simplifying extremely large frequency values into a more practical format. This is especially common in the tech industry, where processor speeds and data transfer rates are often measured in GHz. Our converter performs this simple and accurate conversion instantly using the formula: 1 GHz = 1,000,000,000 Hz.
