Understanding Kilojoules and Kilocalories

What is a Kilojoule (kJ)?

A kilojoule (kJ) is a unit of energy in the International System of Units (SI). It is equal to 1,000 joules and is widely used in scientific contexts and for food energy labeling in many countries.

What is a Kilocalorie (kcal)?

A kilocalorie (kcal) is a unit of energy equal to 1,000 calories. In nutrition and food labeling, the term "calorie" is often used to mean kilocalorie. It's the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius.

Why Convert kJ to kcal?

Converting kilojoules to kilocalories is essential for understanding nutritional information across different regions. While many countries use kJ for food energy labeling, others, like the United States, use kcal (often just called "Calories"). This conversion allows for a consistent understanding of dietary energy intake. Our converter uses the standard conversion: 1 kcal = 4.184 kJ.
