Understanding Joules and Calories

What is a Joule (J)?

The joule (J) is a derived unit of energy in the International System of Units (SI). It is equal to the energy transferred to (or work done on) an object when a force of one newton acts on that object in the direction of the force's motion through a distance of one meter.

What is a Calorie (cal)?

A calorie (cal) is a unit of energy. The "small calorie" or "gram calorie" is the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of one gram of water by one degree Celsius.

Why Convert J to cal?

Converting joules to calories is essential for translating scientific energy measurements into a dietary context. While joules are the standard in physics, calories are more commonly understood in nutrition. Our converter uses the standard conversion: 1 cal = 4.184 J, providing simple and accurate results.
