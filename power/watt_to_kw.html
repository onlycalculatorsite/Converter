Understanding Watts and Kilowatts

What is a Watt?

A Watt (W) is the SI unit of power, equivalent to one joule per second. It is used to quantify the rate of energy transfer.

What is a Kilowatt (kW)?

A Kilowatt (kW) is a unit of power equal to 1,000 watts. It is commonly used to express the power of engines, electric motors, and heaters.

Why Convert Watts to kW?

Converting watts to kilowatts is useful for simplifying large power values into a more convenient format. Our converter performs this simple and accurate conversion instantly using the formula: 1 kW = 1,000 W.
