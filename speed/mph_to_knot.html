Understanding MPH and Knots

What is Miles per Hour (MPH)?

Miles per hour (mph) is a unit of speed in the imperial and U.S. customary systems, expressing the number of miles traveled in one hour. It is commonly used for speed limits and vehicle speeds in the United States, the United Kingdom, and a few other countries.

What is a Knot?

A knot is a unit of speed equal to one nautical mile per hour, which is exactly 1.852 km/h and approximately 1.15078 mph. It is the standard unit of speed used in maritime and aviation contexts.

Why Convert MPH to Knots?

Converting miles per hour to knots is essential for:

  • Maritime and Aviation Navigation: Translating land-based speeds into the standard units used for sea and air travel.
  • Data Consistency: Ensuring that all speed data in a nautical or aeronautical context is in the same unit.
  • Recreational Activities: Helping private pilots or boaters convert familiar speeds into the correct navigational units.
  • Meteorology: Converting wind speeds from MPH to knots for use in weather models and forecasts for maritime and aviation purposes.
Our MPH to Knot converter provides an instant and accurate solution, facilitating these translations.
